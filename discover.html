Why I Chose University of Minnesota Duluth

UMD offered the right mix of academic rigor and close community. The Labovitz School's finance coursework, the strong internship pipeline with local credit unions, and the smaller class sizes made it feel like the best fit. The campus's proximity to Lake Superior and the quality of life in Duluth were deciding factors for me.

I felt welcome during my campus visit — the people were friendly and the facilities (Labs, RSOP, Kirby Student Center) seemed geared toward giving students practical experience. The cost and opportunities to pursue internships locally sealed the decision.

What I Like About Duluth

Duluth blends small-city convenience with immediate access to outdoor activities. It's a place where you can study, work, and unwind without long commutes.

Seasonal Favorites

Fall is my favorite — Enger Tower hikes and crisp days on the Lakewalk. Winter brings Spirit Mountain and cozy study sessions. Summer means Canal Park, festivals, and lake time.

Outdoor & Food Highlights

I love searching for agates on Brighton Beach, grabbing a coffee at a local shop between classes, and trying new food trucks in Canal Park during warm months.

Quick Picks

  • Enger Tower — great sunset views
  • Canal Park & Lakewalk — walks and food
  • Spirit Mountain — winter sports
  • Portland Malt Shoppe — ice cream after finals

Personal Experiences & Campus Highlights

On-Campus

I spend time in the Finance Lab and RSOP rec center. Working at a local credit union supplemented my coursework and gave me practical finance experience. The Investment Club helped me apply theory to practice through portfolio projects.

Off-Campus

Weekends often mean a quick hike, brunch with friends, or catching a live event downtown. Duluth's community feel makes it easy to meet people and build a network while studying.
